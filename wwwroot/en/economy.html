Reform Landmark - Beibu Gulf

The state approved the implementation of the "Guangxi Beibu Gulf Economic Zone Development Plan", the planning period is 2006-2020. Guangxi Beibu Gulf Economic Zone is a key area for China's western development and open cooperation with ASEAN. The establishment of the Guangxi Beibu Gulf Economic Zone is a major decision-making arrangement for the country to implement the strategy of developing the western region, promote the open development of Guangxi, and promote regional coordinated development. The rise of the Beibu Gulf Economic Zone into a national strategy has brought significant historical opportunities to the Beibu Gulf Economic Zone and is a major event in the historical process of reform and opening up in Guangxi.

Unique location - ASEAN Free Trade Zone

"ASEAN" is the "Association of Southeast Asian Nations". By the end of 2002, ASEAN had ten member states. The third largest free trade zone is the largest free trade zone composed of developing countries.

Extending in all directions - a new era

"High-speed rail era - come"

Guangxi became the first minority autonomous region in the country to operate high-speed rail. In the past five years, the railway construction in Guangxi has been “a blockbuster”, with a total of seven high-speed rails and a high-speed rail of 1,751 kilometers, ranking first in the country. Guangxi high-speed rail basically formed a new pattern of modern road network of “Beitong, Nanda, Dongjin, Xilian”.

--At the end of 2013, more than 1,000 kilometers of high-speed rail lines such as Hengliu, coastal and Liunan were opened.

　

--At the end of 2014, Nanguang, Guiguang High-speed Railway and Nanning East Station were opened.

--At the end of 2015, the operation of the Yunnan-Guizhou Railway from Nanning to Baise was opened;

--The whole line was opened at the end of 2016.

--On December 28, 2016, the Nankun Passenger Line was opened. The high-speed railway along the coast of Guangxi entered the Beibu Gulf coastal port and connected to the “Maritime Silk Road”, which formed a large-scale passageway to the southwest, the Pan-Pearl River Delta and the Beibu Gulf.
